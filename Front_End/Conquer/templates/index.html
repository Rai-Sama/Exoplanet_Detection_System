Radial velocity

Orbiting planets cause stars to wobble in space, changing the color of the light astronomer's observe.

Learn more!

Transit

When a planet passes directly between its star and an observer, it dims the star's light by a measurable amount (The method we relied on!)

Learn more!

Direct Imaging

Using large telescopes fitted with adaptive optics and coronagraphs astronomers can take pictures of exoplanets by removing the overwhelming glare of the stars they orbit.

Learn more!

Gravitational microlensing

Light from a distant star is bent and focused by gravity as a planet passes between the star and the Earth

Learn more!

Astrometry

The orbit of a planet can cause a star to wobble in space relative to nearby stars in the sky. This method notices those miniscule changes.

Learn more!
